## 2. Lead paragraphs

Every year, promising students from low-income households earn a place at a Malaysian public institution — Matrikulasi, Foundation, STPM, polytechnic, IPTA or ILKA — only to find they lack the means to make the most of it. This programme exists to close that gap.

The model is straightforward. Members of the community choose to support a student through their studies. It is assistance, not a loan — there is nothing to repay. Our hope is that those who are supported today will, in time, support others tomorrow.

## 5. Can I apply?

## Can I apply?

- **Malaysian citizen** who has just completed, or is completing, SPM or STPM.
- **From a low-income household** — combined monthly income at or below RM5,860, the national B40 threshold (DOSM, 2024).
- **Solid academic record** — at least 5 A's in SPM, or a PNGK of 3.0 or above in STPM.
- **Continuing at a Malaysian public institution** — Matrikulasi, Foundation/Asasi, STPM, IPTA, polytechnic or ILKA.
- **Reachable for a short interview** — usually a 20-minute phone call in English, Bahasa Malaysia, or Tamil.
- **Willing to share progress** — quarterly results and a brief update, so that support can continue through your studies.

**Close, but not exactly?** Apply anyway. Our bar is a little more generous than the headline above — especially on grades — so it's worth applying even if you don't match every line exactly. Applying lets us assess your eligibility; it doesn't guarantee assistance.

## 6. How it works

## How it works

*Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. No fixed deadline.*

1. **Apply**
   Sign in with Google, confirm your HalaTuju profile, and submit. About 5–10 minutes.

2. **We confirm — same day**
   You'll receive an email acknowledging your application on the day you submit it.

3. **Shortlisting — within 48 hours**
   We check your results and household income against the criteria and let you know whether you've been shortlisted.

4. **Complete your profile**
   If shortlisted, we'll ask you to share a few more details and upload supporting documents (IC, results slip, proof of household income). We'll tell you exactly what's needed.

5. **A short interview**
   A brief phone call, usually 20 minutes, in your preferred language. This is to get to know you, not to test you.

6. **Ready for sponsors**
   With your written consent, we prepare a confidential profile for sponsors to consider.

7. **Matched and awarded**
   Once a sponsor chooses to support you, MyNadi Foundation arranges the assistance and administers the funds. From application to award typically takes up to two months.

8. **Staying supported**
   Each quarter, you upload your results and a short progress note. As long as you're progressing, the support continues.

## 7. Questions (FAQ)

## Questions

**Do I need to have used HalaTuju before?**
No. If you're new, the same Google sign-in creates your account. If you've already done the HalaTuju course-matching quiz, we'll use what's in your profile so you don't have to retype anything.

**Is this a loan? Do I have to pay it back?**
No. It is education assistance from a sponsor — nothing to repay, no interest. Our hope is that those who are supported today will, in time, support others tomorrow.

**Is it guaranteed if I qualify?**
No. This is a pilot with limited places and strict criteria. Applying lets us assess you; it does not guarantee assistance.

**Who can see my personal details?**
Your private details — your IC, contact details, and home address — are never shown to sponsors. Sponsors only see a profile, and only after you have given your consent. If you are under 18, a parent or guardian must give that consent on your behalf.

**What documents will I need?**
Only after you've been shortlisted: your IC, your SPM or STPM results slip, and proof of household income or aid (such as your STR letter, an EPF statement, or a payslip). We'll tell you exactly what's needed at that stage — there is nothing to upload when you first apply.

**What levels does it cover?**
Public post-secondary study only — both pre-university (Matrikulasi, Foundation/Asasi at an IPTA or polytechnic, or STPM) and tertiary (IPTA, polytechnic, or ILKA).

**Does it cover private colleges (IPTS)?**
Not in this pilot. The programme supports study at public institutions only. Foundation or Asasi programmes must also be at a public institution (an IPTA or polytechnic) to qualify.

**Is there a deadline?**
Not for this pilot — applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. You'll receive an acknowledgement on the same day you apply, and we aim to confirm shortlisting within 48 hours. From application to being matched with a sponsor typically takes up to two months.

**Is there a cost to apply?**
No. Applying is free and takes about 5–10 minutes.

**Internal notes that do *not* appear on the page:**
- **Income gate (real logic):** STR recipients pass; otherwise eligibility uses *per-capita* household income
  (household income ÷ household size) below RM1,584 — **not** absolute household income. The public copy states the
  simpler "household ≤ RM5,860 (B40)" line and does **not** surface per-capita, to avoid gaming.
  - **Deliberate tradeoff (2026-05-25):** a small, non-STR household at/below RM5,860 can read as eligible but be
    declined on per-capita. Accepted for the pilot — non-STR B40 claims are hard to verify, so STR-or-per-capita is
    the call. Revisit once we have data.
- **Academic bar (real logic, not advertised):** the engine accepts SPM `≥4 at A- AND ≥5 at B+`, or STPM `PNGK ≥2.9`
  — intentionally a notch below the advertised "5 A's / PNGK 3.0" to catch near-misses. The headline stays at the
  stricter figures (under-promise); the "Close, but not exactly?" line invites the near-misses in. The decision is
  **deterministic** — no human/holistic review, and co-curriculum and hardship are **not** decision inputs (they feed
  the sponsor profile + a mentoring flag only), so public copy must not imply case-by-case discretion.
- **Ethnicity is not a criterion anywhere — not even in the pilot.** MyNadi Foundation's Section 44(6) tax-exempt
  status requires the programme not discriminate on the basis of race, so the earlier "Indian-descent pilot" framing
  is dropped from all public copy (supersedes the 2026-05-24 decision to keep it). The engine has no descent check.
