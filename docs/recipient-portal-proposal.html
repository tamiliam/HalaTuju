1Executive summary

Where students who have been awarded assistance live after the application is over.

Today, a sponsored student lands on a single "My Progress" page with three things: log semester results, an optional "share my story" toggle, and an anonymous thank-you to their sponsor. It works — but it is orphaned (no menu links to it) and it stops well short of what running a monthly-assistance programme actually requires: a signed agreement, bank details, payment history, enrolment checks, and a light duty-of-care layer.

This proposes a role-aware "My Assistance" hub with four tabs — Overview, Money, Progress, Support — that fits cleanly into the one-login app shared by course-explorers, applicants and awardees. It is grounded in seven research streams (architecture, cards, payments, student-success, onboarding/legal, navigation, duty-of-care).

2Key decision items

The choices that shape everything else. My recommendation is flagged on each.

① Disbursement rail cost

How recipients get paid monthly.

Recommend: IBG bulk file for the monthly run (cheapest, payroll-style) + DuitNow Name Enquiry to verify the account at onboarding. A prepaid card only later, only if recipients are unbanked or you need spend-control.

② Payments: now or later

Automate the payout ledger soon, or start manual?

Recommend: capture bank details + show a manual payment history first (you pay by normal transfer), automate disbursement in a later sprint behind the existing flag. Lower risk, no live-money code early.

③ Agreement quiz: hard gate or soft

Must the student pass to unlock payment?

Recommend: a hard gate — short, plain-language, and framed as evidence of informed consent (valuable with a young cohort), not a test to fail.

④ Where bank details live

Shared Profile vs the recipient hub.

Recommend: inside My Assistance → Money (recipient-only), not the shared Profile — keeps the common profile clean and the financial data access-scoped.

⑤ Resources scope

How much enrichment content to host.

Recommend: a thin, curated link list with review dates + event-driven nudges — not a content library (the evidence says libraries get ignored and rot).

＋ Mobile navigation (whole app) bigger

A separate, larger decision this surfaced.

Evidence strongly favours a bottom navigation bar over the current top hamburger (~30%+ better feature discovery). Affects the whole app, so decide it on its own — not bundled into this work.

3Fresh ideas & unique features

Beyond the baseline — the things that would make HalaTuju's programme distinctive.

★ At-risk auto check-in unique

Combine signals you already hold — a CGPA dip + a missed semester report + a skipped monthly claim — to trigger an automated, logged nudge offering help. The skipped-claim signal is a disengagement flag most programmes never use.

★ Privacy-preserving transparency

The sponsor sees only a coarse band (on-track / needs-attention / graduated) — never the results slip or identity. Trust without exposure; already the design DNA, worth keeping front-and-centre.

★ Quiz-as-consent, not box-tick

The T&C "agree" step becomes a 3-question comprehension check. It doubles as legal evidence the student understood — and gently teaches the conditions.

★ DuitNow name-check trust badge

Confirm the bank account name matches before the first payout, and show a "Verified ✓" badge. Free via Malaysian rails; kills the #1 cause of misdirected payments.

★ Pay-it-forward flywheel

At graduation, invite recipients to opt in as a future micro-sponsor or peer mentor. Turns beneficiaries into the next cohort's supporters — a community loop few aid programmes build.

★ Milestone moments

Celebrate semester completion, a CGPA jump, graduation — small, timely, human touches that the retention evidence says actually move persistence.

★ Behavioural-default money nudge

For financial literacy, a sensible default (e.g. "set aside RM20/month") beats a course — defaults move young, low-income savers most. Optional, opt-out.

★ Guardian co-portal for minors

For under-18s the guardian is the binding signer (legal necessity) — turn that into a clean guardian acceptance + receipt-of-funds view, not just a name field.

4How it fits the menu (architecture)

One login, three classes (explore → apply → receive) — stages of one journey, not personas.

  • Stable primary nav, content adapts by state — don't make users pick a role; the home Dashboard re-prioritises itself (like StudentAid.gov).
  • Primary destinations, not tabs for the big functions: Home · Courses · My Application · My Aid · More. "My Aid" lights up once sponsored.
  • Tabs only within a destination, capped at 3–4 — inside My Aid: Overview · Money · Progress · Support.
  • Keep the shared Profile single; recipient-only financial data sits in the hub, not the profile.

7Compliance & risk highlights

Minors can't validly sign must-fix

As a private NGO you're outside the Contracts (Amendment) Act 1976, so a minor's direct agreement is void.

The parent/guardian must be the binding signer for under-18s. Have a Malaysian lawyer confirm the structure before launch.

Don't over-build for PCI

PCI governs card data only — it does not apply to bank account numbers.

But bank numbers are more sensitive (static): encrypt at rest, mask to last-4, restrict access, never log. Spend effort on PDPA, not PCI.

PDPA 2024 amendment (in force Jan 2025)

Mandatory breach notification, mandatory DPO, wellbeing data is sensitive (explicit consent), penalties up to RM1m. Your SMS/WhatsApp vendor is a data processor → needs a DPA.

Safeguarding for minors

No unmonitored 1:1 mentor↔minor contact — route all comms through monitored channels (in-app / WhatsApp Business broadcast). Needs a policy + Designated Safeguarding Lead before any mentoring ships.

AML — proportionate, not bank-grade

A grant-maker paying its own beneficiaries is generally not a "reporting institution". Sensible controls: sanctions-screen against MOHA/UNSCR lists, verify identity + name match, keep audit trails.

Clawback fairness

A heavy clawback/bond against a B40 minor risks unconscionability and reputational blowback. Keep any penalties proportionate and plainly explained.

8Research dossier

Seven streams. Condensed findings + sources.

R2 · Prepaid / disbursement cards

  • Swipey is a B2B expense product (built on Fasspay, a BNM e-money issuer) — wrong tool for beneficiary payouts.
  • Cards fit only for the unbanked or when you need MCC spend-control; else DuitNow/e-wallet is cheaper.
  • Dormancy/reload/ATM fees quietly erode small stipends.
  • Minors need a guardian-linked account (Hong Leong Junior, Maybank imteen-i, CIMB YOUth Savers).
  • If displayed: name + last-4 + balance + transactions only (PCI).

Sources: Swipey/Fasspay · HL Junior card · PCI DSS Req 3 · UNHCR cash-vs-card

R3 · Disbursement / payments

  • Two rails: IBG bulk file (cheap monthly batch) + DuitNow Name Enquiry (free, real-time name check).
  • Require a name match before first payout.
  • Failed payments: hard (closed/invalid/name-mismatch → don't retry) vs soft (timeout → retry next batch).
  • AML is proportionate for an NGO; bank account numbers are out of PCI scope but encrypt anyway.

Sources: PayNet DuitNow API · IBG vs DuitNow · Bank-data sensitivity · FATF R8

R4 · Student-success / enrichment

  • Lead with cash certainty + a light human touch, not a content library.
  • Unguided content gets ignored; broadcast nudges ≈ zero effect. Gains come from just-in-time, deadline-tied, trusted-source nudges to high-risk students.
  • Strongest programmes (Posse ~90% grad, Sutton Trust, QuestBridge) win on mentoring.
  • Financial literacy: experiential + behavioural defaults beat workshops.

Sources: Posse · EdResearch — summer melt · Youth financial literacy

R5 · Onboarding & acceptance (legal)

  • Minor's agreement is void for a private NGO → guardian must be the binding party.
  • Clickwrap "I Agree" + audit record is valid (Electronic Commerce Act 2006 s.9) — no licensed digital signatures needed.
  • Use scrollwrap; store timestamp + ID + IP + exact T&C version; version the T&C.
  • Standard clauses: maintain CGPA · semester reports · stay enrolled · use of funds · code of conduct · proportionate clawback · guarantor.

Sources: E-sign Malaysia · Contracts (Amendment) Act 1976 · Clickwrap/scrollwrap · Sunway T&C

R6 · Information architecture

  • State-aware dashboard, not role-toggle; persistent destinations; tabs only for parallel views (cap 3–4).
  • GOV.UK task-list for the application journey; bottom-nav over hamburger on mobile.

Sources: NN/g Tabs · NN/g Mobile nav · GOV.UK Task list · StudentAid.gov

R7 · Engagement & duty of care

  • Two-way SMS nudges work (Georgia State "Pounce": melt 19%→9%) — point them at concrete support.
  • At-risk = multi-signal (CGPA + missed check-in + skipped claim); define the intervention before the detector.
  • Safeguarding + PDPA make mentoring a later, carefully-scoped sprint.

Sources: Georgia State "Pounce" · EAB early-alert · PDPA 2024 amendment · NSPCC safeguarding
