# GUIDE — "Your part in the B40 programme"

### Welcome (intro)
> **Congratulations — and thank you for joining us as a reviewer.**
>
> You're helping the **B40 assistance programme**, which supports students from lower-income
> families to continue into further study. Here's where your part fits:
>
> - Students **apply online** at halatuju.xyz.
> - The system checks eligibility and **shortlists** those who qualify — a B40 background, a solid
>   academic record, and a clear study pathway.
> - Shortlisted students are then **guided to complete** their application: uploading documents,
>   confirming family and income details, and answering any follow-up questions.
> - When an application is ready, it comes to **you** — to check the key facts, talk to the student if
>   needed, and recommend a decision.
>
> This guide shows you how. It takes about 5 minutes.

### 1. Your applicants
On **B40 Applications** you'll see **only the applicants assigned to you** — not everyone's. Each row
shows the name, source, qualification, merit score and status.
`[SHOT 190143 — B40 Applications list — REDACT names]`

### 2. Opening an applicant
Click a name to open their review screen. Everything is on this one page: their details, the checks,
the documents, the interview, and your decision.
`[SHOT 190208 — applicant overview — REDACT name/NRIC/phone/email/address/parent]`

### 3. The four checks
Your review comes down to **four facts**: **Identity**, **Academic record**, **Pathway** (their study
plan / offer), and **Income (B40 need)**. The "Verification verdict" shows what the system is confident
about and what still needs your eye.
`[SHOT 190218 — Verification verdict — REDACT the earner name in the notes]`

### 4. The documents behind each check
The documents the student uploaded are grouped under those same four facts, each with the system's
reading and a status. Click to view a document, or "Re-run" to read it again.
`[SHOT 190432 — Documents grouped by fact — REDACT visible IC/names]`

### 5. The student profile
The **Student profile (draft)** is a short summary written automatically from the student's own
application — a helpful starting point, not the final word. It updates itself as the student answers
questions, and is rewritten into the final version when you save your verdict. You can also expand
**the student's own words** underneath.
`[SHOT 190235 — Student profile (draft) — OK, uses an alias]` · `[SHOT 190259 — the student's own words — optional]`

### 6. Asking the student something
If something's unclear, don't guess. **Raise a query** or **request a document** — the student gets an
email and replies, and their answer appears in the **Outstanding** box. Best done before the interview
so your conversation is focused.
`[SHOT 190317 — Outstanding + Raise a query]` · `[SHOT 190345 / 190404 — Request a document — optional]`

### 7. The interview stage
When you're ready to talk to the student, the **Interview Stage** offers suggested questions (tap
**Suggest interview questions**, or **Generate more** for additional ones). Ask your own too. After
each point, jot **one line** on what you found, then **Submit interview findings**.
`[SHOT 190420 or 190603 — Interview Stage]`

### 8. Your decision
In the **Decision** card you:
- rate the AI's Verification. If you thought the AI got it right, give it a **Pass**, else a **Fail** for each of the facts;
- set a **recommended assistance amount** on the slider (an estimated need is shown to guide you);
- write a short **conclusion**; then
- select **Approve** or **Decline** and finally
- click **Save verdict & generate final profile**.

That records your decision and produces the final profile a sponsor will read.
`[SHOT 190521 — Decision card]` · `[SHOT 190506 — estimated-need note — optional]`

> **Thank you.** Take your time, check the facts, and use a query or the interview whenever you're
> unsure. Your care makes a real difference to these students.

# FAQ

## Administrative

**Is this paid? Do reviewers get any compensation?**
No — reviewing is **voluntary**, and there's no payment. Thank you for giving your time to these
students.

**How do I sign in next time?**
Go to **halatuju.xyz/admin/login** and enter your email and password or sign in with Google.

**How do I change or reset my password?**
On the sign-in page, click **Forgot password**, enter your email, and follow the link we send to set a
new one.

**Does my email have to be Gmail?**
No — any email address works.

**Is the information I see confidential?**
Yes. Treat everything about an applicant as **private** — only discuss it with the HalaTuju team. Please
don't download, share or keep applicant details.

**I'm stuck or something looks wrong with my account — whom do I contact?**
Email the HalaTuju team at **tamiliam@gmail.com** and we'll help.

## Reviewing

**Why do I only see some applicants?**
You see **only the applicants assigned to you**, so you can focus on your own. That's normal.

**What is the "Student profile (draft)"?**
A short summary written automatically from the student's own application. It's a helpful starting
point — always check it against the uploaded documents before relying on it.

**Should I just trust the AI?**
No — the AI gives a *suggestion* for each of the four facts and a draft profile. **You decide.** Your rating 
(based on the documents and your interview) AI with Pass/Fail helps improve AI over time.

**What do the four checks mean?**
Identity (is this really them), Academic record (do the grades match the slip), Pathway (is their study
plan / offer in order), and Income (are they genuinely B40 / in need).

**How do I ask the student a question?**
Use **Raise a query** or **Request a document**. They get an email; their reply shows in the
**Outstanding** box.

**What does "Save verdict & generate final profile" do?**
It records your decision and creates the final, polished profile a sponsor will see.

**What's the recommended amount for?**
It's your suggestion of how much assistance would help, set on the slider. It guides the sponsor.

**The student's offer letter doesn't match their stated pathway — what do I do?**
Note it and ask them in the interview (or raise a query). They may have updated their plan; the
conversation settles it. Or, ask them to upload the latest offer letter, and it would replace the old.

**If the student is already in college, and has completed a semester or two, should I ask for additional documentation?**
If they have completed one or more semesters and have their results (CGPA), you may ask them to upload their
latest result. If it is satisfactory > 3.0, you may consider recommending them for support.
